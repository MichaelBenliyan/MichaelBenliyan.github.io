About Me

UnderGrad

I began learning at University of California, Irvine as a freshman in 2015. I was admitted as a Bio-Medical Physics Major and spent my first year in the hard sciences. Although I was enjoying my classes, after my first year I became interested in pursuing a career in law. I started working at an attorney service office to build firt-hand experience in the industry. Law School Admission

After graduating I began to put together an application for Law School. I set my focus on the LSAT (Law School Admissions Test) and began studying. In October 2021 I scored a 174/180 on the LSAT which is a 99th percentile score and applied to Law Schools shorty after. After a few months I received responses from schools. I was admitted with the President's Scholarship to UF Levine Law, ranked 21 in the country according to USNews, which meant I could attend Law School for only $10,000/year. I had accomplished the goal I had been working towards since my sophomore year of college and yet, I wanted something else.

Projects

Car Racing Game w/ Machine Learning AI

Technologies: NEAT | Python | Pygame

This is a car racing game against a Machine Learning AI trained using the NEAT module in Python. The game supports pixel-perfect collisions and has 3 difficulty setting. Each difficulty setting has 5 levels to complete.

The Machine Learning Simulator took 6 inputs: 5 distance radars measured in 45 degree intervals from left to right and the car's angle. These inputs were linked to only one output: turn right. The fitness function rewarded the cars for every tick they didn't hit a wall but replaced the value with the inverse of their laptime. This was done in order to promote surviving until the AI learned its way around the track, then, the fitness function would be optomizing for fastest laptime around the track.

Spotify Shared Music Room

Technologies: React | Django | Python | Javascript

This project allows users to join shared music rooms in which guests and hosts have shared control over a spotify music account's currently playing music. The application connects with Spotify's API and allows hosts to create rooms where they can control setting such as allowing guests to vote to skip songs and allowing guests to pause the music. The application allows guests to join any room via room-code where they will be able to see the current playing song. The application also supports sessions making rejoining rooms seamless.
